## Premise

# ALPHA v2 — Master Story Bible (The Janaki Variant)

Single source of truth for the v2 run. Supersedes the three v2 files (brief, alpha_v2,
alpha_v2_characters); every contradiction is resolved here, with canon as the final word.

**The one canon departure, by design.** Canon says Janaki is deceased. v2's premise is that she
faked her death and is the living architect. This is the single deliberate deviation, and it is
the whole reason v2 exists. It is handled so the surface canon stays intact: to the world and to
Sita, Janaki is dead, and Sita is named after her dead mother. The faked death is the concealed
truth the film reveals. Outside this one point, canon wins every tie.

**How to feed this to LudLLM.** Part I (Canon) is `user_locked`. Parts II through VI and the
character bibles are the authored layer: `user_seed` for load-bearing choices, `invented` for
revisable scaffolding. Tier the big reveals (Janaki alive, the authored rebellion, the Meridian
principal) as `never_explicit` or `delayed`.

# PART I — CANON (locked, do not change)

Trailer- and studio-confirmed. Two reframings, both permitted by the trailer: Fateh is a grey-zone
antagonist, not a flat villain (the trailer shows Sita turning against him, not that he is morally
simple); and Janaki's death is real to everyone in the world but is secretly staged (the single
sanctioned departure above). Everything else stands.

- **Colonel Sunil Luthra** (Ashutosh Rana). Former Chief of R&AW, a good man, already dead, killed

# PART II — THE ENGINE

Janaki is the surveillance state as a person. She abandoned Sita because she could not protect her
up close, and she has spent nearly two decades building a machine that sees everything so nothing
she loves is ever out of sight again. To sell the machine she must prove the world is unsafe, so she
makes it unsafe. Protection through total control, justified by a manufactured threat. The daughter
she gave away to keep safe is the one person who can stop the machine she built to keep everyone
safe. The wound and the conspiracy are the same shape.

Mythic resonance, free: in the Ramayana "Janaki" is itself a name of Sita (daughter of Janaka).
Mother and daughter carry the same goddess's names. The danger is not only that Sita must fight her
mother; it is that Sita could become her. The film asks whether the weapon turns into the architect.

# PART III — THE DESIGN

**The abandonment, and why Fateh raised her.** Janaki and Fateh were partners in the original
off-books program. When her own enemies closed in, Janaki staged her death and disappeared to make
the infant Sita worthless as leverage. In the gap, Fateh took the child. Her protective abandonment
is the tragedy: she left her daughter in the hands of the one man who would turn her into a killer.
To the world and to Sita, Janaki is dead and Sita was taken (abducted) by Fateh, which is the canon
surface; the hidden truth is the abandonment.

**The PMC and the pivot.** Underground, Janaki founds a private military contractor (placeholder:
Meridian Risk Group) selling deniable force. She learns the real lesson: the leverage is not in
soldiers, it is in data. Meridian pivots from Blackwater to Palantir, from muscle to omniscience, and
now sells predictive intelligence. She is never the public face. A frontman CEO (placeholder: Julian
Mehta, polished, plausible, hollow) fronts it; Janaki is the hidden principal, a name on no document.
Mehta exposed as a puppet is a clean mid-film reveal.

**The product and the prize market.** The system is an all-seeing surveillance and prediction
platform (placeholder: TRINETRA, the third eye, which both sees and destroys). India is the prize.
Ground it in the real debate: NATGRID, the Central Monitoring System, the Aadhaar privacy fights, the
Pegasus scandal. Meridian's pitch is a privatized, supercharged intelligence grid, resisted on
exactly the Big Brother grounds. The sale is stalled, and the stall is the whole motive.

**The false flag.** To break the stall, Janaki engineers a terror attack on Indian soil, Meridian's
name nowhere near it. The craft, the thing that makes it Person of Interest and not a generic bombing:
the attack is built so that afterward TRINETRA can be shown to have flagged the threat the state's own
systems missed. She manufactures both the disaster and the proof that only her machine could have
stopped it. The public trades privacy for safety, the government signs. Keep the attack at narrative
scale; the point is the retroactive sales proof, not the ordnance. The fatal vulnerability is built
in: manufacturing the proof means leaving a thread back to the manufacturer, and that thread is what
Sita and Fateh pull.

**Fateh's thread, and how he learns she is alive.** Fateh, grey but a patriot, has been hunting the
terror outfit Janaki intends to use, not knowing whose instrument it is. He gets close enough to read
the handling and recognizes the tradecraft the way you recognize handwriting, methods only one person
used, a ghost in the methodology. That is how Fateh, alone in the film, realizes Janaki is alive, and
it breaks him, because he mourned her and built Alpha half as her legacy. The woman he honored is the
monster he is chasing.

**Kaul's thread.** Kaul does not know about Janaki. He sees Fateh's off-books, extrajudicial unit as a
liability the government will one day have to answer for, and he moves to shut it down. He is right on
principle and catastrophic on timing: Fateh is the only one hunting the real threat, so every step
Kaul takes to stop the rogue unit clears Janaki's path. Institutional correctness serves the architect.

**Luthra and the origin of Alpha (answering the brief's open questions).** The original off-books
program, the one Janaki and Fateh ran, was sanctioned by Luthra, then Chief: a clean man who did hard
things for the nation, his one shadow. When the program collapsed, Janaki staged her death and
vanished, and Fateh took the infant and continued the work himself, off-books and unsanctioned. The
present-day Alpha is Fateh's rogue continuation. Luthra's stain is having sanctioned the original;
Kaul did not know its history and discovers the current unit as a rogue liability. Neither was the
hidden hand; the institution's connection is Luthra's old sanction. (Canon-safe: Luthra does hard
things for the nation without being corrupt.)

**The web (Janaki's two hands).** Janaki has assets inside R&AW and inside Fateh's unit. Two faces,
two functions, so the conspiracy does not read as ambient corruption:
- **Additional Secretary Arvind Sehgal** (placeholder), her hand inside R&AW: a senior, trusted,
  frightened careerist. He steers Kaul onto Fateh, and his reach into intelligence and tasking is how
  Janaki places targets and pressure where she needs them.
- **Rudra** (new), the intimate mole inside Fateh's unit: a senior operative, Sita's protector-figure,
  turned by Janaki through a moral lever (his guilt over what Fateh did to Sita). He facilitates Sita's
  defection and escape, believing he is freeing her, not knowing he serves Janaki. The intimate
  betrayal that should hurt.

**The antagonist architecture (one villain, three surfaces).** The single true antagonist is Janaki.
Everything else is her surface or her tool: Mehta the visible frontman (mistaken for the enemy until
the strings show), the terror outfit the instrument (which does not know it is being run for a sales
pitch, with a bought faction handler as the seam), and Janaki the hidden hand, revealed in stages.
Fateh is not a fourth pole; he is the grey patriot hunting the same instrument from the other side,
the man who was right.

**The first kill (resolved against canon).** Canon has Fateh hand Sita the assignment at the
restaurant, and that stands. But Fateh does not yet know Janaki is alive, so he cannot have ordered the
kill to protect that secret. The resolution: the victim had found proof that Janaki is alive and was
about to surface it. Janaki needed them silenced, and through her reach she placed the target into
Fateh's stream as a legitimate Alpha lead, so Fateh assigned it to Sita for her first kill, believing
it real. So from her first sanctioned murder, Sita unknowingly silenced a witness to her own living
mother. The gut-punch holds, canon intact, and it is the first thread of the authored rebellion.

# PART IV — THE TWIST LADDER (staged, biggest at the end)

1. **Half-point (Mira).** Mira turns Sita: Fateh weaponized her, her origin is a lie. True, but a
   limited hangout. Reader and Sita together.
2. **Early back half.** Sita learns her mother did not die at enemy hands; the death was staged. First
   crack. Let the reader sit one beat ahead of Sita.
3. **Midpoint.** Janaki is alive, and she is not a victim. She is the principal behind Meridian and
   TRINETRA. The mourned mother is the monster. Mehta is exposed as a front.
4. **The false summit (the Lanka assault).** Sita, Mira, and Kaul storm Fateh's Lanka. It plays as the
   triumph the trailer promises. Then the floor drops: attacking Fateh is exactly what Janaki
   engineered, because Fateh is the one man hunting her operation. Sita has done her mother's work and
   removed the nation's last defense before the attack. The victory was the setup.
5. **The attack and the proof.** The false flag lands. TRINETRA "predicts" it on cue. The sale goes
   through. Sita understands she helped clear the board.
6. **Climax (biggest).** Sita's entire defection, the first kill, the order that sent her to Mira, the
   break from Fateh, the assault, was authored by Janaki from the shadows. Sita was never free; even
   her rebellion was her mother's operation. And the final turn: Janaki abandoned Sita to protect her,
   and that abandonment is what delivered her to Fateh and made her the weapon now standing in the room.
   Janaki built the daughter she is now facing. The personal and the political resolve in one line. Let
   this land as something Sita realizes, not something she is told.

Optional capstone: Janaki built TRINETRA partly to never lose sight of the daughter she gave up. The
all-seeing machine is a mother's surveillance scaled to a nation. The most intimate motive and the most
monstrous one are the same circuit.

# PART V — PLOT DEVICES

- **The manufactured threat, the proof on cue** (the spine). Person of Interest run backward: the
  seller creates the danger and the only evidence that answers it. Forsyth-grade if the corporate
  deniability is airtight and the proof-mechanism is the same act that exposes her.
- **The ghost in the methodology** (le Carre / Forsyth). Fateh identifies Janaki by craft, the
  signature moves only she used, recognition by tradecraft, not a photograph.
- **The puppet principal** (Ludlum). The visible CEO is a hollow front; the real power is a name on no
  filing.
- **The authored rebellion** (Ludlum, the cruelest). Sita's bid for freedom was scripted by the
  villain. Plant it early, the first kill that serves a secret she cannot see, the convenient escape, so
  it pays as horror, not contrivance.
- **The intimate mole** (le Carre). Rudra, close to Sita, betrays out of love. The betrayal is a person,
  not a leak.
- **Exposure as the counter to the eye** (the thematic climax). Sita's weapon against an all-seeing
  machine is not a bigger eye, it is light. The climax is informational: not blowing up TRINETRA but
  making its builder and her method visible, turning the watching back on the watchers. Transparency
  against surveillance, an ending that means something instead of one that explodes.
- **The room-key bookend** (structure). Open on Fateh handing Sita a key, which is a kill. Bookend on a
  key, the access credential to TRINETRA, that Sita now chooses what to do with. The student becomes the
  one who decides. (Note: v2's bookend key is the TRINETRA credential, not a maternal cipher; this is a
  different device from the v1 keepsake, do not import that here.)
- **The first kill, recontextualized** (the gut punch). The victim had found proof that Janaki was
  alive. Janaki placed the target in Fateh's stream; Fateh, not knowing she lives, assigned it to Sita
  as a legitimate kill. So from her first murder Sita was protecting her mother's secret, without
  knowing her mother was alive to be protected.
- **The monastery as mirror** (use Kabir well). Kabir killed his own father-figure for the greater good
  and made peace with it. Sita faces a doubled version: a father who made her and a mother who would
  remake the world into a cage. Kabir is the man already on the far side of that choice, and can be the
  one operative in the universe who has seen Meridian from the other end.

---

# PART VI — THE ENDING (residue, not a clean triumph)

Sita is offered two parents who each protected her by owning her: Fateh, who made her a weapon, and
Janaki, who would make the world a panopticon. The only victory the genre allows at this level is to
refuse both forms of control. Stopping the sale should cost. Expose Janaki and the appetite she fed
does not die with her: the public still wants safety, the next Meridian is already incorporating, and
the frontman model means the head is replaceable. Let Janaki fall or escape, but let the machine she
represents, private power that manufactures fear to sell certainty, outlive her. Sita ends neither
free nor owned. For the first time she chooses, and the choice is the win, not a clean sky.

If the format demands a lift, the honest minimum is that Sita walks out as her own person with
TRINETRA's truth in the open, while the institution that made her and the market that wanted her
mother's machine have learned nothing and are already building the next one.

# PART VII — CHARACTER BIBLES

Each entry ends with an internal-monologue sample. Lift the perception and internal-sample fields into
`build_drafter_context` as the POV's perception filter and voice note.

**Cast geometry.** Fateh and Janaki are two answers to one wound, control as love: he builds a weapon,
she builds a panopticon. Sita's perception is inherited from both, Fateh's lethal read and Janaki's
pattern read, plus the emotional channel both her parents suppressed. Mira is the counter-model
(connection, not control), Kabir the far side of the choice (acceptance), Kaul the institutional foil
to Fateh's zeal. The cast is a closed argument about control.

### Sita, codename Alpha (protagonist)
*Essence: a weapon that was never allowed to become a person, beginning to suspect it has a self.*
- **Origin.** Taken as an infant after Janaki's staged death, raised in isolation and brutal training by
  Fateh, named for a mother she believes the enemy murdered. First sanctioned kill at eighteen, handed
  to her as a birthday gift across a restaurant table; the victim, unknown to her, had found proof her
  mother was alive.
- **Motivation.** Surface: to be worthy of Baba, to do the work clean. Underneath: a starved need to be
  loved for herself and not her use. Once turned: to find out who she is under the weapon, and to refuse
  being anyone's instrument.
- **Worldview.** The world is threats and assignments. Trust is a vulnerability, warmth is a lever
  (taught as tradecraft), people are targets, assets, or handlers. She has never been permitted to
  imagine being a person.
- **Self-image and insecurity.** She thinks of herself as a tool, proud of her competence because it is
  the only thing she has been valued for. Beneath: a child who suspects she is kept only because she is
  useful, that her hunger for tenderness is a defect, and, after she learns she was conditioned, that
  she cannot tell which of her feelings were ever hers.
- **Quirks and habits.** Counts exits on entry. Never sits with her back to a door. Eats fast and
  joylessly, fuel not pleasure. Strips and cleans a weapon to self-soothe. Cannot receive a gift or a
  compliment; the room key poisoned both. Touches the one object that ties her to her mother without
  knowing why.
- **Perception.** Lethal first: threat geometry, exits, hands, weight on the back foot, the most
  dangerous person, the sightlines. Then the trained profiler Fateh built: each person's break point,
  tell, want, fear. Then a third channel she is ashamed of: she notices ease and belonging between
  others, the thing she was never given, and a hunger rises that she shoves down. Meeting someone she
  runs two thoughts at once, how would I put this person down, and, unbidden, would this person ever
  care about me. She distrusts the second.
- **Voice, outer and inner.** Outer: spare, flat, literal, no small talk; blunt and almost childlike
  when feeling breaks through. Inner: clipped, present tense, tactical, pierced by longing she
  suppresses.
  *Internal sample:* "Two exits. The man at the window carries at four o'clock and favors his left. The
  woman is not a threat. The woman is laughing at something small. People do that here, easily, all the
  time. I do not know what it is for. I file the man's weak side and I do not file the laugh and the
  laugh stays anyway."
- **How they think.** Procedural, fast, pattern-matched to training. No vocabulary for emotion, so she
  processes it as malfunction and reaches for tactics when she is drowning. Learning to author her own
  judgment, instead of running Fateh's, is her arc.

### Fateh "Baba" Singh Lakhawat (the grey patriot, the father who made her)
*Essence: a man who loves a daughter and built a weapon and has decided, with full knowledge, that they
are the same act.*
- **Origin.** Old-world intelligence and military, Rajput lineage, forged in proxy wars. Janaki's
  partner in the original sanctioned-but-deniable program. When she "died" and vanished he took the
  infant Sita and built the present, rogue Alpha, half as Janaki's legacy and half as his own
  conviction, raising Sita with a love that is real and a purpose that is monstrous.
- **Motivation.** The nation, above everything, by any means, and tangled inseparably with it, Sita. He
  hunts the terror outfit to land one decisive blow on the enemy, not knowing whose instrument it is,
  and not knowing Janaki lives.
- **Worldview.** The nation is sacred and its survival forgives anything. Its protectors cannot afford
  the sentiment the nation enjoys, though he is full of it. The only sin is weakness before the enemy.
  He is a necessary monster and history will absolve him.
- **Self-image and insecurity.** A patriot and a father who loved Sita properly by making her strong. In
  the smallest hours he knows exactly what he did, has weighed it against the nation, and chosen to be
  unforgivable. His fears: that he is no different from the enemies he kills, that Sita only obeys and
  does not love him, and that Janaki was right about something he cannot face.
- **Quirks and habits.** Daily disciplines, prayer, the epics quoted, old courtesy even to those he is
  about to destroy. One domestic tenderness with Sita, the single true thing between them. Anger
  controlled by a stillness that frightens people more than shouting.
- **Perception.** Strategy and loyalty first. He reads the power structure of a room, who commands, who
  can be turned, resolve or weakness in the spine. Looking at Sita he sees a daughter and an instrument
  at once and will not separate them. Above all he notices loyalty.
- **Voice, outer and inner.** Outer: measured, paternal, courtly, certain, speaks in maxims; tender with
  Sita in a way that is real and controlling at once. Inner: a man forever arguing his own case before a
  tribunal only he can see.
  *Internal sample:* "She did the work clean. Good girl. I made her able to survive a world that would
  have eaten her soft, and the soft ones are in the ground. They will call it cruelty. Let them sit where
  I sit, with the enemy at the gate, and then tell me what mercy costs. I gave my country a sword. I gave
  my daughter a sword. It is the same gift. It has to be the same gift, or I am only a man who hurt a
  child."
- **How they think.** Long-game strategic, rigorous, rationalizing, reducing the present and its people
  to moves in a decades-long war, flawless from one corrupted axiom, which makes him tragic and not
  merely cruel.

### Janaki (the architect; believed dead; the surveillance state in human form)
*Essence: the smartest mind in the film and the most self-deceived, because her entire machine exists to
avoid one feeling.*
- **Origin.** A R&AW officer of formidable analytic gift, Fateh's partner in the original program. When
  her own enemies threatened Sita she staged her death and disappeared to make the child worthless as
  leverage. Underground she built Meridian, pivoted from force to data, and became something larger and
  colder than any flag.
- **Motivation.** Control as protection. She lost everything she could not see or command, and resolved
  never to be blind again. TRINETRA makes the world legible so nothing she values is ever out of sight.
  Selling it needs demand, and manufacturing the threat manufactures the demand. The cold motive and the
  intimate one are the same circuit: a machine so she never loses sight of the daughter she gave away.
- **Worldview.** The world is chaos that can be modeled and managed by whoever sees the most. Sentiment
  is noise, information is power, safety is a product, people are nodes and risk vectors. She has reasoned
  herself out of souls, the only adult in a world of children.
- **Self-image and insecurity.** A realist, a builder, a mother who made the unbearable choice out of
  love, a story she has rebuilt so often it is load-bearing. Buried beneath, where she will not look:
  that she is a coward who abandoned her child and dressed it as sacrifice, that the one variable she
  cannot model is the daughter she gave up, and that this blind spot disproves her whole worldview.
- **Quirks and habits.** Watches before she acts, long silences, speaks last. Never raises her voice,
  which she regards as a failure of control. Obsessive personal data discipline, leaves no trace, while
  stripping everyone else's privacy. Watches Sita on a private feed, a habit both monstrous and
  unbearably sad.
- **Perception.** Panoptic and predictive. She sees patterns, risk, leverage, the variable most likely to
  move, and is almost always right, which has made her contemptuous of the unpredictable. Her one
  perceptual failure is emotional: she cannot read or forecast love, including her own, and Sita is the
  noise that breaks the model.
- **Voice, outer and inner.** Outer: quiet, precise, unhurried, surgically calm; she does not threaten,
  she informs, and delivers devastating things gently. Inner: modeling, predicting, pricing people in,
  with one walled-off region she routes around.
  *Internal sample:* "Mehta is sweating, so the board is sweating, which I priced months ago. The
  minister resists for ninety days and folds on the ninety-first, after the event, when his voters are
  afraid, because people want to be safe more than they want to be free and they hate you for knowing it.
  People are not difficult. There is one node in the world I cannot run, and I keep that file closed, and
  the discipline of keeping it closed is the most expensive thing I do."
- **How they think.** Systems-thinking, probabilistic, ruthlessly rational, with a single region (Sita)
  she refuses to compute. The whole architecture of her brilliance is built to avoid one grief.

### Mira (co-lead, Sharvari's character; placeholder Mira)
*Essence: the one person who sees others as people, which is her gift and her compromise.*
- **Origin and function.** A R&AW field officer under Kaul, grounded, recruited for empathy over cold
  tradecraft. Sent to turn Sita, she collides with real compassion. The "truth" she gives Sita is a
  limited hangout; the deeper truth she does not know is that Janaki is alive and the architect. She is
  genuine, not Janaki's asset; Janaki engineered the circumstances that brought them together, via her
  reach into tasking, without Mira ever knowing she was being used.
- **Worldview.** Everyone has a door, usually loneliness; connection is the strongest tool and the only
  thing worth protecting; cynical about institutions, stubborn about persons.
- **Self-image and insecurity.** Decent, a little too soft and quietly proud of it; fears her compassion
  gets someone killed, and fears worse that she weaponizes real kindness. With Sita she crosses from
  handling into something real and cannot stop.
- **Quirks and habits.** Makes tea as a way in and a read. Remembers the small things and uses them. Light
  disarming touch, talks with her hands, hums when nervous, hides real feeling behind genuine warmth.
- **Perception.** Emotional first. Reads mood and tension on entering, finds the loneliest person in a
  minute, sees the starvation under Sita's competence instantly. Clocks what people need before what they
  want.
- **Voice, outer and inner.** Outer: warm, easy, asks questions, uses your name, self-deprecating to
  lower your guard. Inner: more strategic than the surface, calibrating what you need to hear and whether
  she means it.
  *Internal sample:* "She counted the doors before she looked at my face. Someone taught her that before
  she could walk. I came here to open her like a file and I am sitting here wanting to put my coat around
  her instead, which is the opposite of the job. Pour the tea. Buy a minute. Decide later who you are."
- **How they think.** Intuitive, people-first, narrative. Her blind spot is assuming everyone has a
  reachable human core, the one thing Janaki has buried under a machine.

### Colonel Vikrant Kaul (R&AW chief; trying to stop Fateh)
*Essence: a man whose correctness on principle is, this once, exactly what the enemy needs from him.*
- **Origin and function.** Career R&AW, now Chief running sanctioned deniable operations, a believer in
  the institution and the line. He moves to shut Fateh's rogue unit down. The distinction that keeps him
  from hypocrisy: he runs deniable ops someone can still answer for, and objects to Fateh's unaccountable
  one. He does not know about Janaki, and does not know the rogue unit's full history; he is steered onto
  Fateh by Sehgal, whom he trusts.
- **Worldview.** The state must be guarded from chaos, including its own zealots; accountability separates
  a service from a death squad; he distrusts true believers more than enemies.
- **Self-image and insecurity.** The responsible adult who holds the line and carries what he has signed.
  Fears his caution is sometimes cowardice in principle's clothing, and, by the end, that being right made
  him the real enemy's instrument.
- **Quirks and habits.** Reads any room for liability, asks the avoided question, keeps mental ledgers of
  blowback, dry weary humor, pinches the bridge of his nose, phrases things as questions so people hear
  themselves.
- **Perception.** Consequence first: exposure, the political weather, who answers if this goes wrong,
  where deniability breaks. Reads people for reliability and danger to the mission.
- **Voice, outer and inner.** Outer: measured, ironic, bureaucratically precise. Inner: weary, three moves
  ahead on the political board, doubting his own caution.
  *Internal sample:* "The Minister wants Fateh cleaned up without a fingerprint, which is the same as
  wanting a thing done and not done, and I am the one who explains it to a committee afterward, and no
  answer survives a committee. The old man is about to do something brilliant and unforgivable. Stop him
  before he does it. And try not to think about what he can see that I cannot."
- **How they think.** Institutional, risk-modeling, second-order. His blind spot is the inverse of
  Fateh's: where Fateh ignores blowback to act, Kaul can be paralyzed by it into inaction, and the film
  punishes both halves.

### Major Kabir Dhaliwal (cameo; the mirror)
*Essence: the man already on the far side of the choice Sita is walking toward.*
- **Origin and function.** The universe's foremost operative, who killed his mentor Luthra for the greater
  good and made peace with it. At the monastery he gives Sita and Mira what they need, may fight, and
  recognizes Sita; he can be the one source who has seen Meridian from the other end.
- **Worldview.** The work demands you become something terrible, and you accept it without illusion or it
  destroys you. He has shed the illusions. Stillness, acceptance, the long view.
- **Self-image and insecurity.** Knows exactly what he is and does not flinch, no self-pity, the opposite
  of Fateh. Under the calm, a deep tiredness about whether the paying ever ends.
- **Quirks and habits.** Economy of movement and speech, long unawkward silences, a stillness that reads as
  peace or danger. Notices everything, reacts to little.
- **Perception.** Reads people for whether they have accepted their nature yet. Sees in Sita a younger

# PART VIII — NOTES FOR THE LudLLM RUN

- World stage gets a real plot mechanism with a fatal vulnerability: the false flag is both Janaki's
  lever and her exposure, because manufacturing the retroactive proof means leaving a thread back to the
  manufacturer. Real-org grounding: R&AW, NATGRID, the Central Monitoring System, Aadhaar, Pegasus / NSO,
  Palantir, Blackwater. Clean secret ledger.
- Structure stage gets a four-act shape with a midpoint reversal (the mother is alive and is the
  architect) and the reveal stack in Part IV with explicit reader-vs-character gaps.
- Tier Janaki-alive, the authored rebellion, and the Meridian principal as `never_explicit` or `delayed`.
  Sehgal and Rudra as `hidden`.

  and grand, Janaki modeling and cold, Kaul ironic and weary, Kabir spare, Mehta anxious-shallow, Sehgal
