## Narrative design

- mode: dual_timeline
- POV: dual (2 POV)
- rationale: The world defines two eras that must braid: the 2008 proof Janaki hid is the detonator the 2026 line walks toward, so dual_timeline is structural, not stylistic. The le Carre signature here is the gap between what the reader knows and what Sita does not, and the cleanest engine for that gap is two sealed POVs split across the braid: Sita in 2026 (the blind protagonist, told she was rescued, walking toward danger) and Janaki in 2008 (the conscience who finds the network, hides the proof, and is murdered for it). Janaki's line is the dramatic-irony delivery mechanism: the reader learns through her what Sita cannot know, so every present-day scene is loaded. I keep it tight at two rather than going ensemble because adding Fateh's, Mira's, or Sehgal's head would buy a Forsyth hunter-track at the cost of the secrets that must stay delayed (Fateh grey, the trap, the limited hangout) and would let the reader see the mole directly, which the rules forbid (Sehgal caught by a tell, never shown). Mira, Kaul, Fateh, and the offscreen principal are all rendered through Sita's tight present POV; the hunter-and-hunted pressure is carried by the dual-track manhunt within that POV rather than by a second present voice.

## Tropes in play

- **The mole at the top** (subverted): Sehgal is the foreign principal's deepest penetration of R&AW, sitting over clandestine financing and tasking. The reader is steered to suspect the rot is internal early via the braid and the false-flag signature, rather than a whodunit-among-five.
- **The agency as antagonist** (subverted): Kaul's accountable, decent service is the obstacle, hunting the one man fighting the real enemy. There is no single corrupt director; the betrayal is emergent, the sum of reasonable incentives Sehgal nudges, so every good instinct (accountability, compassion, caution) serves the rot.
- **The false flag** (subverted): Mira flips Sita under a calibrated partial truth (the abduction). The recruiter is sincere and unwitting; the deception is institutional, not personal cruelty, and the murder of Janaki is itself a killing staged to read as enemy action, the network's domestic signature.
- **The plot-of-plots** (subverted): The pipeline trap the reader follows is a wrapper around Sehgal's quieter operation to have India's own service destroy Fateh. The inner op rhymes ethically with the outer, so the clues read as ethical in hindsight, not just as a twist.
- **The making of the spy (paternal betrayal)** (subverted): Sita's capacity for deception was built by Fateh from an abducted child. But the formative betrayal is grey, not monstrous, and the very thing done to her becomes the instrument that finds the rot and saves the country.
- **The handler-protege bond** (subverted): Fateh and Sita are the deepest relationship in the book, paternal intimacy that the tradecraft forces toward betrayal. No sentimental redemption: the bond is real and instrumental at once, and she lets him believe his conditioning still works.
- **The single slip that unravels everything** (subverted): Sehgal, flawless for thirty years, knows one detail tied to Janaki that no clean officer should. Planted early as an apparent strength, it is caught not by dogged record-checking but by Sita's inherited instinct for the buried lie.
- **The intimate enemy / mirror antagonist** (subverted): Fateh (heat) and Sehgal (cold) mirror each other, both needing Janaki's murder buried, enemies who never collude. The win tastes like loss: Sita prevails by destroying the one man who saw clearest.
- **The procedural set-piece** (subverted): The retrieval of Janaki's analog proof is walked through step by step, with one analog step that can only be done in person. The inversion: the macguffin is the protagonist herself, the living cipher who carries the key worn since infancy.
- **The hollow victory**: The mission succeeds and the win is ashes: the rot is exposed, Fateh is dead and partly right, and Sita is left with the residue of having used her enemy's logic to destroy a patriot. Used straight as the thematic close; the reader is never allowed to cheer.

## Acts (4)

## Act 1: The Weapon in Motion  `a1`

Four acts, because this story's reversal is a hard turn that earns its own boundary: burying it in a three-act middle would soften the moment the ground shifts under Sita, and five would dilute a single converging operation. Act one opens on Alpha competent and lethal, the tradecraft working, so the reader trusts the surface before it is pulled apart. It plants the bonds that will break (Sita and Baba; the institution's belief that Alpha is the threat) and sets Kaul's accountable service hunting the rogue unit. The 2008 braid opens on Janaki catching the network at inception, so the reader holds a truth Sita does not. The inciting fracture is Mira's contact and her calibrated partial truth: Sita was abducted, not rescued. Midpoint reasoning (the spine, landing at the a2/a3 boundary): it combines le Carre and Forsyth, delivering a name AND a deadline at once. The le Carre turn is internal, the hunt turns inward, it is one of us, the service is being steered; the Forsyth turn is external, the activation clock becomes visible, the network moves the instant Fateh falls. That single beat reframes everything before it and starts the run to the crisis.

Beats:
- Cold open: Alpha executes a flawless deniable kill; the tradecraft works
- Fateh hijacks the Golden Crescent pipeline, scaling the war chest and setting the bait
- 2008 braid opens: analyst Janaki catches the foreign network at its inception
- Inciting fracture: Mira makes contact and tells Sita she was abducted, not rescued

## Act 2: The Crack Widens  `a2`

Rising complication. Sita rationalizes near-misses as bad luck and low-level leaks while Mira's limited hangout takes root and she begins, for the first time, to read her own handler. The hunt for Fateh tightens, unknowingly steered by Sehgal's control of money and tasking. Sita and Mira seek Kabir at the monastery, the mirror who already killed his own father-figure for the greater good. The 2008 line accelerates to its tragedy: Janaki encodes the proof's location into the infant's keepsake, Luthra buries her discovery for reasons of state, and she is killed in a murder dressed as enemy action. By the act's end the reader has connected Janaki to Sita as mother and child, ahead of Sita, so the present line is fully loaded with irony.

Beats:
- Mira's partial truth takes root; Sita starts reading Baba for the first time
- 2008: Janaki encodes the proof's location into the infant's keepsake and hides the analog file
- 2008: Luthra buries the discovery; Janaki is murdered in a strike staged to read as enemy action
- Kabir gives Sita and Mira what they need; the tell points the rot inward, to the home service

## Act 3: It Is One of Us  `a3`

Opens at the midpoint turn and hardens doubt into certainty. The direction goes inward (the service is being steered, the false flag is the tell that the rot is domestic) and the clock appears (the starved network activates the moment the lure collapses). Sita learns Alpha's real target and that Fateh is a grey patriot, not a madman, and that the pipeline is bait. The act's spine is the procedural retrieval of the dead woman's file: the keepsake cipher decoded, the 2008 proof physically carried out by paper, the one analog step that can only be done in person. The home truth lands where it costs most: Janaki was her mother, and her sanctioned first kill at eighteen was the cover-up of that murder. The act closes on a false summit, an apparent win that collapses and exposes the steering hand.

Beats:
- Midpoint: the hunt turns inward (it is one of us) and the activation clock becomes visible, a name and a deadline at once
- Sita learns Alpha's true target and that Fateh is the patriot, not the threat; the pipeline is a trap
- Procedural retrieval: the keepsake cipher is decoded and the 2008 proof is physically carried out
- Home truth: Janaki was her mother; her first kill buried that murder
- False summit collapses, exposing that a hand inside the service has been steering the hunt

## Act 4: The Price  `a4`

The crisis and the cost. The convergence night, the Lanka sweep, as the alarmed network moves to reclaim the pipeline. Sita's own decisive discovery is placed where it hurts most: she catches Sehgal's single human tell, a timing or a name no clean officer should know, the detail thirty flawless years could not erase, caught not by procedure but by the inherited eye her mother left her. She is the only person alive who could see it. The price is paid in the same breath as the vindication: Fateh dies and is partly right, so Sita carries that she destroyed the one man who saw clearest. The room-key bookend closes the circle on her first kill. Victory is technically complete and emotionally void, residue, not relief.

Beats:
- The convergence night: the network moves to reclaim the pipeline and exposes itself
- Sita catches Sehgal's one tell, the detail no clean officer should know
- Fateh dies, vindicated and unforgivable in the same breath
- The room-key bookend; vindication without relief
